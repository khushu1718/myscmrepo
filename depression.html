DEPRESSION

ABOUT DEPRESSION

Persistent sorrow, a gloomy mood, a loss of interest in formerly enjoyable activities, and other symptoms that impair with everyday functioning characterise clinically severe depression.

Genetics, family history, trauma, stress, and sickness are all known to play a role in the development of the syndrome. It's one of the most common mental illnesses, affecting roughly 7.1 percent of all adults in the United States each year.

Fortunately, effective therapies such as medicines, psychotherapy, or a combination of the two are available. Self-help techniques can also assist people in better coping with their symptoms and feeling better.

MOOD DISORDER

A mood disorder refers to a broad class of mental health conditions, including depression and bipolar disorder, that primarily impact emotional states. People may experience prolonged periods of a significantly depressed or sad mood, or they may have episodes of an abnormally elevated or irritable mood to an extent that it causes distress and interferes with daily living.

Types of depression

  • Major Depressive Disorder(MDD)
  • Persistent Depressive Disorder(PDD)
  • Bipolar Disorder
  • Postpartum Depression(PPD)
  • Premenstrual Dysphoric Disorder(PMDD)
  • Seasonal Affective Disorder(SAD)
  • Atypical Depression

Symptoms of depression

  • Depressed Mood
  • Decreased Interest
  • Changes in Appetite
  • Sleep Disturbances
  • Psychomotor Disturbance
  • Fatigue
  • Feelings of Worthlessness or Guilt
  • Difficulty Concentrating
  • Recurrent Thoughts of Death

Causes of depression

  • Stress
  • Grief and Loss
  • Poor Nutrition
  • Family History & Genetics
  • Circadian Rhythm Disturbances

Treatment For Depression

  • Psychotherapy
  • Interpersonal Therapy
  • Cognitive Behavioral Therpy
  • Social Skills Training
  • Psychodynamic Therapy
  • Supportive Counseling
